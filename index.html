Academic & Professional Background

I completed my master's degree in Health Policy and Economics at Weill Cornell Graduate School of Medical Sciences in 2016, followed by a PhD in Health Services Research with a concentration in Health Economics from Brown University in 2022, where I also served as a postdoctoral research associate.

My research program addresses critical challenges in U.S. healthcare: the consequences of vertical integration between insurers and providers, and how payment models drive disparities in care delivery. What distinguishes this work is the integration of rigorous academic training with nearly a decade of senior leadership experience in healthcare economics and value-based payment strategy at some of the nation's premier accountable care organizations and health insurers.

This industry experience at leading healthcare organizations provided unique operational insight into how payment policy reforms shape organizational behavior. I witnessed firsthand how these entities respond to value-based payment incentives: optimizing quality performance, refining clinical condition capture to maximize risk adjustment, and implementing strategies to manage utilization and costs. These insights inform research questions that bridge the gap between policy intent and real-world implementation, enabling me to examine not just whether reforms work, but how and why they produce their observed effects.

Technical Expertise

I maintain expert-level proficiency in Python, R, Stata, and SAS, supported by nearly a decade of intensive experience working with large-scale healthcare databases, including administrative claims data, electronic health records, and post-acute care assessments. My technical expertise extends to designing and implementing both local and cloud-based SQL and NoSQL database architectures for enterprise-scale datasets. Additionally, I bring substantial experience leading multidisciplinary teams of programmers and analysts in complex health services research and healthcare analytics environments.
